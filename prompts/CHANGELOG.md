Hypothesis partially confirmed: C4 and C2 moved in the predicted direction; C1 did not because Rule 1 forbids direct answers but not facts embedded in questions — Output 1 continued leaking dates and events as scaffolding. C3 regression was an unintended consequence: tightening to 2–3 sentences and front-loading Rule 10's diagnostic crowded out causal/comparative depth, especially in Scenarios 4, 8, 11, 15. Worst performers: Scenario 11 (17/32, Watts Towers misconception — Output 2 directly corrected the student), Scenario 5 (18/32, edge label "." — Output 1 dropped five historical facts), Scenario 7 (19/32, Chavez Ravine — both outputs stated the displacement fact directly). Output 2 outperformed Output 1 on C1 in 11/17 scenarios, revealing that the rules are landing inconsistently across samples. | Applied 2026-04-23 |
| v2 | Rule 1 hardened: forbid historical facts in any form (declarative, rhetorical, parenthetical, embedded in questions). Rule 2 tightened: single closing question, no stacking. Rule 15 added: protect analytical depth under the sentence cap — short responses must do more work in the closing question, not less.
