# User Testing Notes (Anonymized)

This file contains anonymized observation notes from the four user testing sessions conducted for LA History. All sessions followed the protocol in [`user_testing_protocol.md`](user_testing_protocol.md). Sessions ran approximately 20–25 minutes each. Participants are identified only as P1–P4; no real names, emails, or other identifying information appear anywhere in this file.

## Participant Summary

| ID | Demographics | Background | Session Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Male, college student | Grew up in Los Angeles | ~25 min |
| P2 | Female, college student | Did not grow up in Los Angeles | ~22 min |
| P3 | Female, college student | Did not grow up in Los Angeles | ~24 min |
| P4 | Female, college student | Grew up in California (not LA proper) | ~20 min |

The recruitment criteria asked for high school or college age learners interested in LA history. All four participants were adults (18+) who had given written consent. Two had grown up in or near LA; two had not. This roughly matches the target persona split (the tool is aimed at non-natives, but native participants surfaced assumptions the design takes for granted).

## P1 — LA Native, College Student

**Tags:** `quiz-pacing`, `edge-direction`, `chat-underuse`, `tutorial-clarity`

### Task 1: Explore and Visit

- Clicked Kuruvunga Springs first. Read the location description and the historical timeline aloud to himself before attempting the first quiz.
- Asked why there was a lag after getting a quiz question correct.
- Got 4/4 on the first quiz, mentioning that he guessed the last question based on prior US-history knowledge (about Spaniards). Noted he was "looking at the things that stuck out, like the definition of the springs."
- **Suggestion:** put a limit on how far the user can zoom in.
- Moved to Ballona Wetlands next. Read the description and timeline. Got the third quiz question wrong on the first attempt and noted that the explanation after the wrong answer was helpful. Passed at 75%, guessing the last question.
- Then visited San Gabriel Valley Foothills. Read everything, attempted the quiz. **Observation he flagged himself:** "most questions fall under the category of recognition rather than recall." Said hints didn't load (timed out).
- Said the background music made it feel "like playing Minecraft."
- Reported that the cursor occasionally turns white when clicking on a location and asked us to fix it.
- Submitted the concept map and went back to the map, but the Spanish-era locations were still locked. Source of confusion.
- The Memory Challenge feature did not work for him.
- Did not check the dashboard or settings during play. Afterward, said the dashboard was useful for seeing progress and suggested moving the badges to the bottom of the dashboard.

### Task 2: Build the Concept Map

- Went through the concept-map tutorial quickly.
- Added all 3 locations. Then went back to read the tutorial again.
- Visibly squinted and scratched his head trying to recall the relationships.
- Said "idk" out loud when the AI tutor responded to him adding a relationship between the Springs and the Foothills.

### Task 3: Get Help from the AI

- First reaction to a tutor response: "idk."
- Went back and forth between locations trying to find connections.
- Final score: 85/100.
- Frustrated that the feedback mentioned he was supposed to explain the edges, since he hadn't been told to.
- Important misconception he revealed: he thought connections were **bidirectional**, based on how he interpreted the AI feedback.
- After reading the concept-map AI feedback, said: "I guess the feedback was kinda helpful."

### Exit Questionnaire (paraphrased)

- Helpfulness: "mildly helpful." Did not use the chat unprompted.
- Confusion: edge directionality and the era-lock confusion after submitting his first concept map.
- One change: clearer signal that connections have a direction and what that direction means.

## P2 — Non-LA, College Student

**Tags:** `tutorial-clarity`, `text-volume`, `chat-mixed-feelings`, `forced-learning-positive`

### Task 1: Explore and Visit

- Created an account easily; said it felt like her data was protected.
- Confused that the app started with only 3 unlocked locations.
- The website tutorial did not pop up at first, leaving her unsure what to do.
- Session was at night and she was not in a good headspace to read carefully. Skipped the location descriptions and went straight to the quiz, guessing answers.
- **Feedback:** "too much text," wants important information bolded.
- Suggested removing em-dashes because "they make the text clearly look like AI wrote it."
- Did not like the music.
- Felt the emoji use made the app "look like it was made by AI."
- Overall, said it was "a good way to interact and learn."

### Task 2: Build the Concept Map

- Had to click the question-mark icon to find the tutorial; what to do wasn't obvious.
- Did not want to read the tutorial — "too many words." Felt instead of clicking through different steps, "there should be one big popup telling you the steps you're meant to take."
- Once she figured it out, understood the task was about connecting regions.
- Said she felt that even if she had read the location descriptions, building connections would still have been hard.
- Asked the observer mid-task: "is the AI looking for a specific set answer for the connections?"
- Liked the AI feedback that came after submitting the map, though it was delayed.
- **Strong positive comment:** appreciated that the app forced her to think and make connections.
- Did not like constant feedback after each connection — preferred working alone, then asking for help on her own terms.
- The Memory Challenge feature was broken (HTTP 500); when it eventually opened it would not let her submit answers.

### Task 3: Get Help from the AI

- Said the AI suggestions were helpful and contained the right content from the stories.
- Liked that the feedback forced her to consider answers in other ways.
- Felt overall there was too much text on screen.
- Said the AI helped her recall the stories.
- **Tension she expressed:** the AI was helpful in principle, but felt like it was steering her toward a specific direction she wasn't thinking about, which made her assume there was a single right answer.

### Exit Questionnaire (paraphrased)

- Helpfulness: liked it but did not use it often.
- Confusion: tutorial was inadequate and instructions were too verbose.
- One change: prefers asking questions on her own terms vs. constant guidance.

- Confused about why only three locations were unlocked. Tried clicking on all three to see if that would unlock more.
- Liked the information but had trouble "locking in" — attributed it to either being late or to history not being her preferred subject.
- Liked the music and thought the text-to-speech feature was cool.
- Did the San Gabriel Valley Foothills quiz, was confused about the answers, got the quiz wrong. Tried again expecting a different question set; got the same one. **Quote:** "How is this teaching instead of making users memorize?"

### Task 2: Build the Concept Map

- Made her way to the concept map but spent a long time reading the tutorial instructions and was still slightly confused after.
- Said she felt overwhelmed by the number of options on the site, with too many instructions adding to the confusion.
- Repeatedly tried to get hints from the observer about how to use the concept map.
- Eventually placed all three locations on the concept map but had trouble building connections.
- Mentioned how deep the connections needed to be — felt like too high a bar.
- Did **not** use the chat unprompted.
- The labels and nodes she added were vague; she did not initially use the chat even after being told she could.
- Eventually used the chat and said our ideas (the AI's responses) were "cool."
- Treated the chat like a generic chatbot — short questions like "why" and "ok."

### Task 3: Get Help from the AI

- Asked the tutor a few questions about San Gabriel.
- Was impressed by the rapid responses.
- Felt the tutor was "just trying to get her working on the map again instead of answering."
- Closing reflection: she expressed admiration for the site but said she was confused multiple times. Notable quote: **"the instructions make sense to you guys because you made them."**

### Exit Questionnaire (paraphrased)

- Helpfulness: minimal — only used when prompted.
- Confusion: tutorial felt designed for someone who already knew the app.
- One change: simplify the onboarding and make instructions feel less assumed-knowledge.

## Cross-Participant Patterns (Synthesis)

These are the patterns identified across at least three of four sessions. They were the basis for the design changes documented in the final report.

### Pattern 1 — Edge Directionality Was Not Self-Explanatory

**Evidence:** P1 explicitly thought connections were bidirectional. P2 asked the observer if the AI was "looking for a specific set answer." P3 left her labels vague. P4's "seashells" connection was creative but had no direction implied.

**Implication:** the concept map UI signals that an edge exists but does not signal what the edge **means** in either direction.

### Pattern 2 — Participants Did Not Use the AI Chatbot Naturally

**Evidence:** All four participants. P1, P2, P3 only used the chat after the task script prompted them. P2 explicitly stated she preferred to ask for help on her own terms. P3 treated the chat like a generic GPT-style search box. Only P4 engaged the chat without coaching, and she rated it 3.5/5.

**Implication:** the Socratic design assumes active engagement, but the placement and framing of the chat does not invite it. Students approach it passively.

### Pattern 3 — Tutorial and Instruction Volume Was Too High

**Evidence:** P2 ("too many words"), P3 ("too many steps"), P4 (tutorial popped up at random times). P1 reread the tutorial more than once.

**Implication:** the tutorial pattern is wrong (multi-step popovers) for this audience. A single overlay or progressive disclosure would land better.

### Pattern 4 — Participants Wanted Different Levels of Guidance

**Evidence:** P2 explicitly preferred asking on her own terms. P4 explicitly asked for a browse mode with no quizzes or unlocks. P1 wanted clearer signposting. P3 wanted less verbose instructions.

**Implication:** the current design imposes a single learning path on all users. A more flexible structure (browse vs. tutor mode) would accommodate the different motivations participants brought to the app.

---

## Design Changes Made in Response

Documented in detail in §5.3 of the final report. Summary:

1. **Re-scoped the AI tutor to the concept map only.** Originally available everywhere; now restricted to the concept-map context. Forces task-relevant interaction.
2. **Added edge-direction reminders to the system prompt.** The tutor now asks clarifying questions about directionality before commenting on historical content.
3. **Redesigned the tutorial overlay.** Single screen with dimmed background and arrows instead of multi-step popovers. Skip-tutorial button added for returning users.
4. **Shortened the concept-map tutorial text.** Reduced to essential steps only.

A planned but not-yet-implemented change for v2.0 is **Browse Mode** — addressing P4's explicit request and P2's preference for self-directed exploration.
