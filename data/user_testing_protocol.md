## User Testing Protocol

### User Consent Form:

Do not record audio/video without separate written consent.   
[https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeB\_3boYwqN15IaFo6httiHGdy2DAGg8YpDuF6UDCNieaEo1g/viewform?usp=dialog](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeB_3boYwqN15IaFo6httiHGdy2DAGg8YpDuF6UDCNieaEo1g/viewform?usp=dialog)

### Make sure the environment has:

- A quiet room with a laptop/desktop computer (participant may use your device)  
- Screen visible to you (the observer)  
- No other people present besides the participant (except for the 1-2 observers)

### Before the session starts, tell the participant:

"*We're testing a history learning app, not testing you. There are no right or wrong answers. Please think out loud as you go, say whatever is on your mind, even if it seems obvious. We won't help you unless you get completely stuck on a technical issue. We want to see what the app does, not what we think it should do*.”

Do **not** explain what the AI tutor is supposed to do. Do not mention "Socratic" or "concept map" before they encounter these features.

### Task Script:

Give each participant these tasks in order. Read them verbatim. Do not add examples, hints, or clarifications unless the participant is blocked by a login or technical error (not confusion about the task itself).

#### Think-Aloud prompts (use only if the participant goes silent):

- “What are you thinking right now?”  
- “What did you expect to happen when you clicked that?”  
- “Was that response helpful? Why or why not?”

#### When to Intervene (emergency only)

- If the app crashes or the tutor gives a completely nonsensical response, say, “That’s a bug, our fault. Let me refresh, and you can continue”. 

#### Task 1: Explore and Visit

*"This is an app about Los Angeles history. Take a few minutes to look around and visit any locations that interest you. There's no wrong place to start."*

**What to observe**: How they navigate the map, whether they read location descriptions, and how they respond to the era lock system, and whether the UI seems intuitive

#### Task 2: Build the Concept Map

*"You should see a Concept Map tool for the era you've unlocked. Open it and try to build a map that shows how the places you visited are connected. Use the chat panel on the right side if you want."*

**What to observe:** Whether they use the chat unprompted, how they respond to the tutor's questions, whether they add edges or only nodes, and what labels they write.

#### Task 3: Get help from the AI

*"Ask the chat panel something about the era you're working on, whatever is on your mind about the map or the history."*

**What to observe:** What they ask, whether the tutor's response makes sense to them, whether they follow up or disengage, and whether they express frustration or surprise.

### Exit Questionnaire

1. **Helpfulness:** "On a scale of 1 to 5, how helpful did you find the AI chat panel while building the concept map? (1 \= not helpful at all, 5 \= very helpful) Why did you give it that number?"  
2. **Confusion points**: "Was there any moment where you weren't sure what the app wanted you to do, or where the AI's response didn't make sense? Describe what happened."  
3. **One change:** "If you could change one thing about the AI tutor, what would it be?"  
4. **Overall**:  "Is there anything else you want to say about the experience?"

### Debrief & Analysis:

*Complete this section after all sessions are done.*

#### Step 1 — Pattern identification

*Review all four participants’ notes. Look for behaviors or responses that appeared in 2 or more participants. Record at least 2 patterns below.*

#### Step 2 — Design change log

*For each pattern that suggests a design problem, document the change made in response. At least 1 change is required.*

#### Step 3 — Summary statement

*Write 2–3 sentences summarizing the most important finding and the change made. This text will be used directly in the M4 and final report.*
